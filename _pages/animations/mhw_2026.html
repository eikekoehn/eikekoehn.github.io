The marine heatwaves off of France in 2026

In June 2026, the global ocean sea surface was warmer than ever before for this period of year in the satellite observational record, i.e., since the 1980s. The figure below shows the sea surface temperature (SST) anomalies relative to a climatology (1991-2020) on June 27, 2026 (based on OISST data, Huang et al., 2020; Banzon et al., 2016; Reynolds et al., 2007). While some regions show colder than usual conditions, the map is dominated by red colors, indicating that waters are warmer than on average in the 1991-2020 period. The onset of the expected El Niño is showing by the widespread anomalous warm conditions in the Eastern Tropical Pacific. But also the waters around Central Europe show high anomalies. In the following we will zoom into the waters around France.
